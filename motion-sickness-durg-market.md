<p><p>The motion sickness drug market comprises three primary types: anticholinergic, antihistamines, and others. Anticholinergic drugs, such as scopolamine, work by blocking neurotransmitters that trigger nausea. Antihistamines, including dimenhydrinate and meclizine, counteract symptoms by inhibiting histamine receptors, effectively reducing motion-induced discomfort. The "others" category includes alternative treatments like natural remedies and non-pharmacological solutions. These categories address the diverse needs of consumers, offering various options for preventing and treating motion sickness across different demographics and settings.</p></p>
