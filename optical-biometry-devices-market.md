<p><p>The optical biometry devices market is divided into contact and non-contact types based on their operational methods. Contact type devices require direct contact with the patient's eye, providing precise measurements often in clinical settings. Non-contact type devices, on the other hand, use light waves or imaging technologies to gather data without physical contact, enhancing patient comfort and reducing the risk of infection. Both types are essential for accurate eye measurements, aiding in diagnostics and treatment planning.</p></p>
