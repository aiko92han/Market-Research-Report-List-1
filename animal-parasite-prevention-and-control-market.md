<p><p>The animal parasite prevention and control market consists of three key types: topical, injection, and oral medications. Topical medications are applied directly to the skin, providing localized treatment against external parasites like fleas and ticks. Injection medications offer systemic protection by delivering active ingredients directly into the bloodstream, often ensuring a longer-lasting effect. Oral medications are ingested, targeting internal parasites such as worms, and are often favored for their ease of administration and thoroughness in treatment.</p></p>
