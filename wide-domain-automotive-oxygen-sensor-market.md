<p><p>The wide domain automotive oxygen sensor market primarily includes two types: titanium oxide and zirconia types. Titanium oxide sensors detect oxygen levels based on changes in resistance when exposed to varying oxygen concentrations, making them effective in reducing emissions. In contrast, zirconia sensors utilize a ceramic material that generates a voltage in the presence of oxygen disparities, allowing for precise air-fuel ratio control. Both types are essential for optimizing engine performance, improving fuel efficiency, and ensuring compliance with environmental regulations in vehicles.</p></p>
