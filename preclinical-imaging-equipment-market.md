<p><p>Preclinical imaging equipment encompasses various techniques to visualize biological processes in living organisms. Bioluminescent or fluorescent imaging uses light-emitting markers to track cellular activity. CT (Computed Tomography) or PET (Positron Emission Tomography) imaging provides detailed anatomical and functional information through advanced radiological techniques. Ultrasound imaging utilizes sound waves to capture real-time images of internal structures. Other market segments may include MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) and hybrid imaging technologies, broadening the scope of preclinical research capabilities.</p></p>
