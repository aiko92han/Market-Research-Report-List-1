<p><p>Digital biomarkers are objective, quantifiable data collected from digital devices to assess health. The market is divided into two main types: passive and active digital biomarkers. Passive digital biomarkers continuously gather data without user intervention, such as tracking sleep patterns or physical activity through wearables. In contrast, active digital biomarkers require user interaction, like inputting symptoms through an app or responding to questionnaires. Both types play crucial roles in personalized healthcare, enabling continuous monitoring and enhanced patient engagement.</p></p>
